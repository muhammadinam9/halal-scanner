About Halal Scanner

Halal Scanner is a free tool that helps Muslims check whether a food or cosmetic product is permissible to consume. Scan a product's ingredient label with your phone camera, scan its barcode, or paste the ingredients, and the app screens every ingredient against Islamic dietary law — labelling each one halal, haram, or doubtful (mashbooh) with a short, plain-English reason for anything that needs verifying.

Key features

Scan printed ingredient labels using on-device text recognition; look up packaged products instantly by barcode; get a clear halal, haram, or doubtful verdict for every ingredient; tap any ingredient to see what it is and its common sources; and read the explanations in multiple languages, including English, Urdu, Hindi, and Arabic.

It is built for Muslim shoppers, parents, and anyone following a halal diet who wants a quick second opinion while shopping. Halal Scanner is an ingredient-screening aid for your own research — it is not a fatwa or a halal certification. Always verify with the manufacturer and rely on recognized halal certification before consuming.

Before you begin

Halal Scanner is an ingredient-screening tool to assist your own research. It is not a fatwa, a halal certification, or a substitute for guidance from a qualified Islamic scholar. Ingredient rulings — especially “doubtful” items — depend on the source, which can vary by manufacturer and region. Results may contain errors from label reading (OCR) or automated assessment. Always verify with the manufacturer and rely on recognized halal certification before consuming. You use this app at your own discretion and responsibility. We collect anonymous usage statistics to improve the app.
